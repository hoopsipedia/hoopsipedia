# Hoopsipedia Vision & Mission

## Mission
To be the definitive historical database and analytical authority for college basketball — the place fans, media, and analysts turn to first when they want to understand the game's past, present, and future.

## Vision
KenPom owns efficiency. Torvik owns predictive modeling. Hoopsipedia owns **history, rankings, and the definitive record.**

Nobody owns that space in college basketball. We're planting our flag.

## What We're Building
Hoopsipedia is not just a stats site. It's the complete college basketball encyclopedia — 360+ Division I programs, every season ever played, every tournament run, every head-to-head rivalry, all verified against the NCAA official record book.

We are building toward a proprietary ranking system — the Hoopsipedia Ranking — that reverse engineers the strongest predictive signals from NET, KenPom, BPI, Sagarin, and every other major metric, drops the noise, and combines the best of the best into one definitive number. A number worth trademarking. A number that becomes part of the college basketball lexicon.

## Our Advantages
- **Historical depth** that no one else focuses on — all-time records, program histories, head-to-head matchups across decades
- **The comparison engine** — Winsipedia-style program comparisons that don't exist for basketball anywhere else
- **The Blue Blood Index** — a proprietary composite ranking of all-time program strength that will generate debate, engagement, and traffic
- **NCAA official records as our source of truth** — no inflated numbers, no disputed totals, just the real record
- **Coaching era analysis** — quantifying the difference between programs that attract greatness and programs that manufacture it. KenPom measures the NOW. Hoopsipedia measures **institutional basketball DNA.**

## The Playbook
1. Launch during the NCAA Tournament — the single biggest traffic event in college basketball
2. Fans are searching for matchup histories, program stats, Cinderella stories — that's our traffic
3. Roll out proprietary rankings that generate debate. Debate generates traffic. Traffic generates authority
4. Media members start citing us. Podcasters reference our rankings. We become part of the conversation
5. We become the go-to. We become a verb. "Check the Hoopsipedia ranking."

## The Standard
KenPom started as one person with a spreadsheet who saw the game differently. Now his name is a verb in college basketball. There is no reason Hoopsipedia can't carve out that same space.

We have nothing to lose and everything to gain.
